What is a Private Limited Company in India?

A private limited company in India is a distinct legal entity characterized by limited liability, where the shareholders' liability is restricted to the amount invested in the company. This business structure ensures a clear separation between personal and corporate assets. In a private limited company, ownership is divided into shares held by a limited number of shareholders. Unlike public companies, shares in a private limited company cannot be freely traded on the stock market, fostering a more controlled and closely-knit ownership environment.


Operational flexibility is a key feature, allowing for efficient decision-making processes. The company must have a board of directors responsible for strategic decisions and overall management. Financial information is typically more confidential than public companies, as private limited companies are not obligated to disclose detailed financial data to the public. This structure is popular among small to medium-sized enterprises seeking a balance between limited liability protection and operational autonomy.

Types of Private Limited Companies

  • Company Limited by Shares: This is the most common form of a private limited company. The liability of its members is limited to the amount unpaid on their shares. In the event of liquidation, shareholders are only liable for the nominal value of their shares.
  • Company Limited by Guarantee: Unlike a company limited by shares, this type does not have share capital. Instead, members provide a guarantee, specifying the amount they will contribute in the event of winding up. Such companies are often prevalent in non-profit and charitable organizations.
  • Unlimited Company: In this type, there is no limit on the liability of the members. Each member is personally responsible for the company's debts. While less common due to the increased risk for shareholders, it provides greater flexibility in financial matters.

Private Limited Company Compliances

After your Private Limited Company Registration in India, it is subject to several compliances that must be fulfilled regularly. These include

Annual General Meeting (AGM)

Holding an AGM within the prescribed time frame, where financial statements, reports, and other matters are presented to the shareholders.

Financial Statements

Preparing and filing annual financial statements, including balance sheets, profit and loss statements, and cash flow statements, as per the requirements of the Companies Act.

Audit of Financial statements

Conducting an annual audit of the company's financial statements by a qualified auditor and filing the audited reports with the authorities.

Compliance with Income Tax and GST

If applicable, comply with the company's tax obligations, including income tax return filing and GST return filing.

Secretarial Compliance

Appointing a qualified company secretary and ensuring compliance with secretarial requirements, such as maintaining necessary records, filings, and compliance certificates.

Annual Filings

Making timely filings with the Registrar of Companies, such as the annual financial statements, annual returns, and other required forms and documents.

FAQ on
Private Limited Company Registration

How to register a startup company in India?

Registering a startup company in India is not too difficult, but it does require certain steps to be followed. The process involves obtaining Digital Signature Certificates (DSC), Director Identification Numbers (DIN), filing of incorporation documents with the Registrar of Companies (ROC), and obtaining a certificate of incorporation. This can be done online or offline, depending on the preferred registration mode. Additionally, certain compliances need to be met regularly after registration. With the help of a professional service provider, registering a startup can be made easy and hassle-free.

Private Limited Company Registration Process

Private limited company registration involves several steps, including obtaining a digital signature (DSC) and DPIN, name approval, and filing the SPICE form. In addition, the company must file e-MOA (INC-33) and e-AOA (INC-34) and apply for a PAN and TAN. Incorporation through SPICE is the default option for most companies. Cleartax can assist with private limited company registration, including guiding required documents and associated costs.

How many days are needed for pvt ltd company registration?

If all the documents are ready, it will take around two weeks or 15 days for Private Limited Company Registration.

How much does it cost to start and run a private limited company?

It can be divided into four parts: money needed to set up a private limited company, accounting, auditing, compliance, and miscellaneous expenses. Approximately a minimum pricing of Rs. 40,000 is needed and a maximum of any extent depending on the business.

Can Foreign Nationals or NRIs become Directors in a Private Limited Company?

Yes, NRIs or Foreign Nationals can become Directors of a Private Limited Company and be a majority shareholders of the company. But importantly, at least one Director on the company's Board of Directors should be an Indian Resident.

Can a Private Company hold multiple businesses?

Yes, a Private Company can hold multiple businesses, but it should be mentioned in the company’s MoA and approved by a registrar.

Can I have a registered place for the company at the residential place?

Yes, you can have a registered place of a Private Company in a commercial and also in a residential place by submitting sufficient proof. A company's registered office is where the business gets official communication from the MCA or any concerned authorities. The address will also be displayed on the Ministry's portal.

What is the Annual Compliance required to be fulfilled by a Private Company?

During every financial year, the private limited company must hold one Annual General Meeting (AGM) and about four board meetings such as once every four months. Further, an independent auditor must regularly audit financial statements and the company bank statement. Later, it shall file form AOC – 4 and MGT – 7 for Annual Compliance within the given time.

Can Private Limited Companies float equity or shares to the general public?

No, a Private Limited Company cannot float equity or shares to the general public.

The registration of a Private Limited Company is done online or offline?

The procedure for Private Limited Company registration in India is completely done online. Before implementing the Companies Act 2013, offline and online facilities were available. However, to achieve the objective of digital India, the government has made this procedure entirely online.

What is meant by Pvt Ltd Company Registration in India?

Private limited company registration in India means creating a legal identity and bringing into existence of a separate entity to carry on the desired business. A certificate of incorporation of the company shall be issued by the Registrar of Companies, Ministry of Corporate Affairs, as evidence of the registration of the Company.

Does a Private Limited Company Registration require a Physical Address or Premise?

Yes, a Private Limited Company needs a physical location as all communication with the Company by various Government authorities will be done to such physical location only.

What is the Private Limited Company Registration Certificate Lifetime?

The Private Limited Company Registration Certificate is valid for a lifetime as long as the Company complies with all the necessary rules and regulations and files the required documents on time. However, suppose there are any changes to the Company's structure or management, such as a change in directors or shareholding pattern. In that case, the necessary filings must be made with the concerned authorities.

After Private Limited Company Registration, What are the compliances I should follow?

After Private Limited Company Registration, there are certain compliances that you need to follow. These include maintaining statutory registers, filing annual returns and financial statements, conducting board and general meetings, and complying with other regulatory requirements. Non-compliance can result in penalties, fines or even legal action against the company and its directors. It is, therefore, important to ensure timely compliance with all relevant laws and regulations.

What are the other types of Companies in India?

Apart from Private Limited Company Registration, several other types of companies exist in India. These include Sole Proprietorship, Partnership Firm, One Person Company (OPC), Public Limited Company, and Limited Liability Partnership (LLP).

What is the authorised capital required to start a new company?

The authorised capital required to start a new company depends on the type of company and the nature of its business. For instance, in the case of a Private Limited Company Registration, the minimum authorised capital required is Rs. 1 lakh. However, this does not mean the company must raise this amount immediately upon incorporation. It simply indicates the maximum amount up to which the company can issue shares to its shareholders.

Can I register a trademark without a company?

Yes, you can register a trademark without having a company. Trademark registration in India is governed by the Trade Marks Act 1999 and can be done by individuals, companies, or any other legal entity.

Who is the Registrar of Companies (ROC)?

The Registrar of Companies (ROC) is an office under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) responsible for administering and regulating the companies registered in India. The ROC's primary responsibilities include maintaining records of all companies, approving company names, verifying and registering documents related to company registration and compliance, and ensuring that all companies comply with relevant laws and regulations.

OPC vs PLC

One Person Company (OPC) and Private Limited Company (PLC) are two types of companies in India. OPC is a type of company that can be registered with only one member, whereas PLC requires at least two members for incorporation. OPC has limited liability protection, but the owner controls the company's affairs. On the other hand, PLC has more stringent compliance requirements and is suitable for larger businesses looking to raise capital from investors. Ultimately, the choice between OPC and PLC depends on the business's nature and goals.
